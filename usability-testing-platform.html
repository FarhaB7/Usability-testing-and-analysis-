Executive Summary
The checkout flow presents significant usability barriers that likely contribute to cart abandonment. With a SUS score of 62 (below the 68-point "Good" baseline), users experienced frequent confusion during coupon entry, payment form validation, and multi-step navigation. Five critical issues were identified that directly prevented task completion for 29% of participants.
01 Key Findings
🔴 Critical Finding #1
Coupon field is invisible: 6/8 participants were unable to find the promo code field without assistance. It was collapsed under a small "Have a promo?" link below the fold, causing 75% to abandon the discount attempt.
🟡 Critical Finding #2
Unclear progress indicator: Participants couldn't determine how many steps remained. 5/8 expressed surprise when a new step appeared. The current design doesn't show step labels, only a generic progress bar.
🟢 Critical Finding #3
Payment error messages are confusing: When a credit card number fails validation, the error message says "Invalid entry" without specifying which field failed or why, causing an average 2.3 additional attempts per participant.
🔵 Positive Finding
Product images in cart are effective: All 8 participants confirmed their items correctly using product thumbnails in the order summary, suggesting cart visibility is working well as a trust signal.
🟡 Finding #5
Address autocomplete is appreciated: When it works, address autocomplete saved significant time. However, it failed silently for 2 participants with PO boxes, causing confusion.
"I didn't even know there was a promo box. I just gave up and paid full price."
— Participant 3, Task 2
02 Design Recommendations
Priority Recommendation Addresses Effort Impact
P0 — Critical Expose the promo code field — Display it prominently in the order summary sidebar, always visible, not collapsed Finding #1 Low Very High
P0 — Critical Redesign step indicator — Replace the generic progress bar with labeled steps (1. Cart → 2. Shipping → 3. Payment → 4. Review) with step names visible Finding #2 Medium Very High
P1 — High Improve error messages — Specify exactly which field has an error and why (e.g. "Card number must be 16 digits — you entered 15") Finding #3 Medium High
P1 — High Fix autocomplete for PO boxes — Either support PO Box autocomplete or gracefully fall back to manual entry with clear messaging Finding #5 Medium Medium
P2 — Medium Add persistent order summary — Keep a collapsed/expandable order summary visible on all steps, not just the final review page General UX High High
P3 — Low Email field de-duplication — Pre-fill the payment page email if already captured during account creation or earlier steps Redundancy Low Medium
